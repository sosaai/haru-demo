OMAKASE · お任せ

I leave it
to you. 任せる

The chef chooses everything — the fish, the order, the pace. You choose only how long you want to sit. Three rhythms, written this morning, told piece by piece.

WHAT IT MEANS

A counter dinner
without a menu.

Omakase begins when the chef bows and the first piece arrives. There is no "next page" — only the next piece. You eat each one in two bites, in silence if you like. The pace is set so that the warmth of the rice and the temperature of the fish meet exactly at the moment they reach the counter.

HOW WE SIT

A few things to
know before.

01 · Seating

Eight at the counter

Two seatings: 19:00 and 21:30. The counter is l-shaped, eight stools, you face the chef directly.

02 · Etiquette

Use your hands

Nigiri is finger-food in its proper home. Dip the fish — not the rice — into a little nikiri. We do the rest.

03 · Pace

Eat in two bites

Each piece is built for the moment it lands in front of you. The next one comes when your plate is empty, not before.

04 · Dress

Smart casual

No dress code — we ask only no strong perfume. The fish has a quieter voice than your cologne.

Tanaka-san pours the soy sauce for you. He brushes the nikiri himself. He times the rice to the second. After two hours you understand why every great sushi room in the world has only eight seats.
